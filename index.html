ABOUT SCOTT

Scott Siller, 45, was introduced to fishing as a young child growing up on a cattle ranch in Wyoming. He would travel with family to Cook Lake for rainbow trout, and to neighboring rivers and streams for brook trout. In the early 1990s, Scott moved to Wisconsin to seek a career in Law Enforcement. In 1995, he started his career with the Milwaukee Police Department. During a late night shift, his squad partner invited him to go bass fishing. It was from that point on that bass fishing became a passion.

In 1996, Scott purchased his first bass boat and started competing in local club and evening tournaments. After several successful years competing in club tournaments and local opens, Scott decided to enter the Professional division of the B.A.S.S. Bassmaster Opens. Scott still attends off-season meetings and supports his local Wisconsin Bass Nation club, Bass Anglers Ltd., in Milwaukee, Wisconsin.

Scott is always looking for his next big challenge - fishing new waters and competing against the best anglers in the world has made him the fisherman he is today. Fishing new and diverse waters has also made him a very versatile fisherman. Fishing is not only a passion for Scott, itâ€™s a true dedication and priority. He has taken what he has learned over the past 19+ years of bass fishing and he has mentored and coached rookie anglers who are anxious to learn from an experienced angler. Despite the fact that Scott has competed in the Bassmaster Opens, he prides himself in his involvement with his local bass club and positively representing tournament anglers in the local communities that he visits. Scott aspires to qualify for the B.A.S.S. Elites and take the title for the Bassmaster Classic one day.
